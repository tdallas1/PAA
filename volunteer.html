Why Volunteer With PAA?

Make a meaningful impact while gaining valuable experience

Volunteering with Pediatric Advocacy Alliance offers a unique opportunity to combine your passion for helping children with valuable hands-on experience. Whether you're pursuing a career in healthcare, education, social work, or simply have a heart for service, PAA provides a supportive environment where you can develop skills, build relationships, and create meaningful change.

Our volunteers come from diverse backgrounds but share a common dedication to improving children's lives. As a volunteer, you'll work alongside like-minded peers and professionals, gain insight into pediatric healthcare challenges, and directly contribute to programs that support vulnerable children and families in our community.

How to Get Involved

From application to active volunteering in four simple steps

1

Submit Your Application

Complete our online volunteer application form with your contact information, availability, areas of interest, and relevant experience or skills. We welcome volunteers from all backgrounds and experience levels!

2

Meet Your Volunteer Coordinator

Once you’re signed up, a Volunteer Coordinator will reach out to welcome you and help match you with upcoming events and opportunities that fit your interests.

3

Get Briefed

Before your first event, we’ll share everything you need to know, like safety tips, event details, and how to make the most of your impact. It’s short, simple, and done via email or text. If you signed up to volunteer closely with children at external sites (like hospitals or clinics) your Volunteer Coordinator will walk you through any extra requirements, such as vaccination records, background checks, or site-specific forms.

4

Begin Your Volunteer Journey

Start making a difference! Just come ready to help and bring good energy. You'll be connected with your volunteer team and begin contributing to PAA programs. Ongoing support and additional opportunities will be available throughout your volunteer experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about volunteering with PAA

Do I need prior experience to volunteer with PAA?

No prior experience is required for most volunteer roles! We welcome students from all backgrounds and provide comprehensive training for each position. Your enthusiasm, reliability, and commitment to our mission are the most important qualifications. Some specialized roles may benefit from specific skills or experience, but we have opportunities for everyone regardless of background.

What is the minimum time commitment for volunteers?

We ask for a minimum commitment of 3-4 hours per month for at least one semester (about 4 months). This ensures continuity in our programs and allows you to have a meaningful volunteer experience. Many of our roles offer flexible scheduling to accommodate student academic calendars. Some specialized positions or committees may require additional time commitments, which will be clearly communicated during the application process.

Are there any requirements I need to fulfill before volunteering?

All volunteers must complete an orientation session and role-specific training. Depending on your volunteer position, you may also need to complete a background check (provided at no cost to you), provide proof of certain vaccinations (especially for hospital-based roles), and complete child safety training. These requirements help ensure the safety and well-being of the children and families we serve.

Can I receive academic credit or service hours for volunteering?

Yes! Many volunteers use their PAA experience to fulfill service-learning requirements, practicum hours, or community service credits. We're happy to complete any necessary documentation for your academic program. Please let us know about your specific requirements during the application process, and we'll work with you to ensure your volunteer experience aligns with your academic goals.

What support will I receive as a volunteer?

PAA is committed to supporting our volunteers throughout their experience with us. You'll receive thorough initial training, ongoing guidance from experienced team leaders, regular check-ins, and opportunities for additional skill development. We also host volunteer appreciation events, offer letters of recommendation for dedicated volunteers, and provide networking opportunities with healthcare professionals and community organizations.
