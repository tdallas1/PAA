Our Journey

Key milestones and events that have shaped our organization

November 2024

Donation Drive Launch & Community Collaboration

This 2024 winter, PAA marked a major milestone in its growth with our first official Donation Drive, organized in partnership with River Forest United Methodist Church. What began as a simple flyer campaign blossomed into a full-scale community effort; connecting volunteers, neighbors, and partner organizations around a shared mission of support.

Our volunteers hit the streets, going door-to-door with flyers and heart, spreading the word about this new initiative. It was one of our earliest outreach efforts, and one that truly shaped who we are today. For many of our members, this was their first time engaging with the public on behalf of PAA. It sparked something special: real connection, a sense of purpose, and even friendships that still exist today. Together, we collected dozens of donations that were distributed to Sarah’s Inn, Cradles to Crayons, and Lurie Children’s Hospital bringing warmth, dignity, and care to families across Chicagoland.

This drive didn’t just fill donation bins; it became a turning point for our organization. It taught us the power of grassroots work, the impact of collaboration, and the kind of momentum we could build when people believed in our cause.

December 2024

Social Work Fair Showcase

Our first time presenting at the Dominican University Social Work Fair was a turning point for PAA. With board members and our advisor, Dr. Scherrer, proudly representing us, we introduced dozens of students, faculty, and professionals to our mission: advocating for the health, rights, and futures of children. For many who stopped by, it was their first time learning about pediatric health advocacy as a student-led movement and they were eager to get involved.

This fair gave us the platform to not only network, but also plant seeds for future partnerships and spark conversations that continue to shape our work today. We met future volunteers, collaborators, and supporters who saw in PAA a cause they could stand behind.

December 2024

Jingle Bell Run

PAA participated in the annual Jingle Bell Run to support children’s health and raise awareness for arthritis research. It was our first off-campus event as a group, and even though we didn’t know what to expect, it brought us closer.

We walked, ran, took way too many photos, and spent the morning supporting a cause bigger than ourselves. It’s a tradition we hope to keep because sometimes, showing up together is the impact.

February 2025

Happy & Healthy Hearts Workshop

This workshop served as a cornerstone in our development of student-led pediatric health education. With guidance from physician assistant Elena Petrusevska, participants not only explored foundational clinical skills but also engaged directly with children and families in a hands-on learning environment.

The event combined service with experiential learning. Students practiced taking heart rates, demonstrated proper handwashing, and facilitated nutrition-focused activities. It highlighted PAA’s unique role in blending advocacy, healthcare awareness, and early clinical exposure, setting a precedent for future interactive programs designed to uplift both youth and aspiring healthcare professionals.

March 2024

April Baby Showers Kick-Off Event

The April Baby Showers Kick-Off marked a significant moment in PAA’s mission to support young families across Chicagoland. What began as a creative, community-building event quickly transformed into one of our most impactful donation campaigns to date. Students came together to make over 20 personalized baby bibs, assemble heartfelt scrapbooks, and write dozens of encouragement notes for mothers and newborns.

But beyond the crafts and care, this event catalyzed a wave of generosity because within days, we collected hundreds of essential baby items from the campus community alone. This event laid the foundation for our Bundles of Joy initiative and affirmed the power of small acts multiplied through community.
