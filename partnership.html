Partner With Us

Creating Meaningful Change Together

At Pediatric Advocacy Alliance, we believe that the most meaningful impact happens through collaboration. By partnering with organizations, businesses, healthcare facilities, and educational institutions, we amplify our collective ability to support children and families in need. Our partnerships are built on shared values, mutual respect, and a common goal of creating positive change in pediatric healthcare and advocacy.

Partnership FAQs

Common questions about partnering with PAA

What is the minimum commitment for a partnership?

We design partnerships to be flexible and mutually beneficial. There is no standard minimum commitment, as each partnership is customized based on shared goals and capacity. Some partners collaborate with us on single events, while others engage in ongoing initiatives throughout the academic year. We're happy to discuss what arrangement would work best for your organization.

How can my organization financially support PAA?

Financial support can take many forms, including program sponsorships, event underwriting, material donations, or general operating support. As a student-led organization, we operate efficiently with low overhead, ensuring that financial contributions directly support our pediatric advocacy initiatives. We can provide detailed information about specific funding opportunities and recognition benefits for your organization.

Can PAA students provide volunteer services at our facility?

Yes! Many of our partnerships involve PAA student volunteers supporting pediatric-focused initiatives at partner facilities. Our students come from diverse academic backgrounds and can assist with patient activities, health education, family support, and special events. We work closely with host organizations to ensure that volunteer activities meet both your needs and our educational objectives while always prioritizing the well-being of children and families.

What training do PAA student volunteers receive?

All PAA members receive foundational training in child advocacy principles, cultural competence, and professional conduct. Depending on their specific volunteer assignments, students may receive additional specialized training in areas such as patient interaction, child development, health education, or specific health conditions. We also work with partner organizations to incorporate any site-specific training requirements into our preparation process.

How does PAA handle confidentiality and privacy concerns?

We take confidentiality and privacy extremely seriously. All PAA members receive training on confidentiality practices, and we comply with HIPAA and other relevant privacy regulations when working in healthcare settings. We establish clear agreements with partner organizations regarding information sharing, photo/video permissions, and other privacy considerations. Our students understand the importance of maintaining the dignity and privacy of the children and families we serve.

Can we partner with PAA for a one-time event?

Absolutely! While we value long-term partnerships, we're always open to collaborating on meaningful one-time events that align with our mission. Whether it's a health fair, awareness campaign, fundraiser, or special program for pediatric patients, we can work with you to design and implement successful events that meet shared objectives.
