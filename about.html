About Us

Our Story

How we began and where we're headed

Building a Community of Advocates

Pediatric Advocacy Alliance (PAA) was founded in 2024 by a visionary group of student leaders who saw the urgent need for a unified space where students could champion the health, rights, and well-being of children, especially those in underserved communities. What began as an idea between peers quickly grew into a dynamic, student-led movement committed to real-world advocacy.

From the start, PAA has been rooted in an interdisciplinary approach. Our community brings together students from medicine, public health, social work, education, business, STEM, policy, and more; reflecting the reality that caring for children takes all of us. We believe that lasting impact is made when we combine lived experience, academic knowledge, and collective action.

Today, PAA continues to grow in reach and influence. We equip our members with the tools to lead service-based projects, raise awareness around key pediatric issues, and work in partnership with healthcare professionals and community organizations. From hands-on volunteering to institutional advocacy, we are building something bigger than ourselves: a culture of student-driven, child-centered change.

Our Journey

Key milestones in our growth

Fall 2024

Official Founding

The Pediatric Advocacy Alliance officially launched in October 2024 at Dominican University, founded by Trinity Dallas and led by a team of student advocates committed to pediatric health and justice. The organization was recognized by Student Life and guided by founding mentors Dr. William Zic, Assistant Professor of Nursing and practicing pediatric nurse, and Dr. James, a senior professor of social work with decades of experience in child welfare. The inaugural board established PAA's mission, branding, and first semester of programming.

Fall 2024

Building Identity and Momentum

PAA quickly established a strong brand voice, a clear mission, and a sense of purpose that resonated across campus. With 30+ members from nursing, social work, business, biology, and beyond, the organization grew fast and forged early collaborations with student groups and community partners. With trusted mentors in place and a bold vision for interdisciplinary advocacy, PAA laid the foundation for lasting impact.

Winter 2025

Turning Point in Growth and Impact

What started as a student-led initiative became a recognized force on campus. During this period, PAA restructured its executive board, doubling leadership capacity and formalizing systems for volunteer tracking, programming, and internal communication. More importantly, the organization expanded its footprint—building new partnerships with clinics, connecting with academic departments, and gaining recognition from campus leadership. Winter 2025 marked a shift from a start-up club to a well-organized, high-impact alliance with both structure and reach.

Winter 2025

Advocacy Training and Skill Building

PAA launched its first advocacy training sessions to equip members with foundational knowledge in pediatric justice, health policy, and service-based leadership, laying the groundwork for future action campaigns.

Winter 2025

Expanding Presence and Recognition

Through service projects, clinic partnerships, and high-impact events, PAA quickly became a visible and trusted presence both on campus and in the surrounding community. Students, faculty, and partners alike began to see PAA as a leading voice in pediatric-focused advocacy and outreach.

Winter 2025

Academic Integration and Organizational Expansion

PAA officially partnered with Dominican University's Community-Based Learning (CBL) program, becoming a designated volunteer site for nursing students completing civic engagement coursework. This milestone not only strengthened the organization's academic ties but also accelerated its need for growth. In response, PAA began expanding its executive board, increasing event capacity, and laying the groundwork for signature annual programming to meet rising interest, demand, and impact.

Fall 2025

Rooted in Our Home, Focused on What's Ahead

At our home, Dominican University, PAA will expand its efforts both on and off campus to empower the next generation of healthcare leaders, educators, social workers, and policymakers. Through awareness campaigns, signature events, and service-driven outreach, we'll continue building community through service—while creating space for students to lead with purpose and advocate for change. Looking ahead, PAA will remain a driving force for pediatric justice at Dominican and beyond.

Our Values

The principles that guide our work

Community

We believe in the power of connection and collaboration, bringing together diverse perspectives to create meaningful change in pediatric healthcare.

Empowerment

We equip students with the knowledge, skills, and confidence to become effective advocates for children's health and well-being.

Advocacy

We use our collective voice to advance policies, practices, and systems that prioritize children's health needs and rights.

Compassion

We approach our work with empathy, understanding, and a genuine commitment to improving the lives of children and families.

Equity

We strive to address healthcare disparities and ensure that all children have access to quality care regardless of their background or circumstances.

Learning

We foster a culture of continuous learning, growth, and professional development among our members.
